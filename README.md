## The role shift

| The old title | Is becoming | What changed |
|---|---|---|
| Product Manager | **AI Product Engineer** | PMs who ship AI features themselves; prompts + evals + prototyping |
| Marketer / SDR | **GTM Engineer** | Revenue workflows automated with code + AI |
| Data Analyst | **Analytics / RAG Engineer** | Wiring trusted data into AI systems |
| Recruiter | **AI Sourcer** | AI-driven sourcing + outreach at scale |
| Solutions Engineer | **Forward-Deployed Engineer** | Embeds with customers, ships custom AI |

> Why it matters: roles requiring AI skills carry a **~56% wage premium** (up from ~25% a year earlier), and AI Engineer was a **#1 fastest-growing** title with postings up ~143% YoY.

## The new AI-native roles

- **AI Engineer** — builds on LLMs: RAG, agents, evals, inference.
- **AI Product Engineer** — owns product *and* builds the AI features.
- **GTM Engineer** — automates go-to-market with code + AI.
- **Forward-Deployed Engineer (FDE)** — customer-embedded builder.
- **RAG Engineer** — retrieval pipelines + grounding for accuracy.
- **Context Engineer** — designs what an AI model knows and when.
- **Agentic AI Specialist** — designs autonomous multi-step workflows.

## FAQ

**Do I need an ML PhD to get an AI-native job?**
No. Most AI Engineer and AI Product Engineer roles want people who can **ship** with LLMs and APIs — RAG, agents, evals — not researchers. Demonstrated projects beat credentials.

**What's the fastest way to stand out?**
Build 2–3 real projects (see the [projects repo](https://github.com/landedjobs/projects-to-land-an-ai-job)) and get **referred** rather than applying cold — referrals convert far better than the application pile.

**Are these roles remote / available in India?**
Yes — the job lists include US, remote, and India-based roles.
